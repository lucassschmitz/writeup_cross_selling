## Summary

### Credit Registry Policy Research Discussion

Lucas and Andrew met to discuss Lucas's research interests in credit registry policies, specifically focusing on changes in Chile over the past 15-20 years. Lucas explained his interest in policies affecting information available in credit registries and the entities participating in these registries, mentioning specific examples such as the deletion of defaults for amounts below $5,000 and information from unemployed individuals.
### Credit Registry Information Interpretation

Andrew and Lucas discussed how lenders interpret missing information in the credit registry, which only contains negative information about defaults. Lucas clarified that the registry doesn't provide a complete credit report but focuses specifically on reporting bad events, so the absence of information isn't meaningful. Lucas also mentioned that laws have changed regarding which entities participate in the registry, including retailers with credit cards, and there are efforts to add positive information to benefit consumers who pay on time.
### Registry Information and Banking Models

Lucas explained that the registry in question applies to both businesses and individuals, similar to credit bureaus in the United States. He discussed a financial model by Shaffer regarding adverse selection in banking, where incumbent banks have better consumer information than entrants, affecting market competition. Lucas outlined his idea for a structural paper using exogenous variation to identify parameters and evaluate optimal registry information provision.
### Credit Expungement Research Model

Andrew and Lucas discussed a research model focused on tracking the impact of expunged bad credit events on individuals' ability to borrow or obtain mortgages. Lucas explained that he would have access to data from a credit registry, including information on commercial theft, credit card loans, mortgages, and current account balances, which could be used to observe changes after law modifications. The discussion centered on potential research questions, such as how expunging bad credit events might affect individuals' access to loans and mortgages.
### Open Banking and Financial Inclusion

Lucas discussed his research on open banking and information sharing, highlighting a potential negative impact on financial inclusion. He noted that policymakers have not fully considered how increased information sharing could affect banks' lending decisions and potentially limit access to credit for some individuals. Lucas mentioned that while there are some existing studies on this topic in Chile, they are primarily reduced-form papers without a structural model. Andrew expressed interest in Lucas's research approach and suggested it could contribute valuable insights to the policy discussion.
### Credit Access Research Discussion

Andrew and Lucas discussed a research paper involving credit access and information framing, which Andrew noted is of interest to both finance and macroeconomics.
